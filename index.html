Urban Infrastructure

Smart Cities Demand Smarter Security

The Challenge: Evolving Security Operations Needs

  • Legacy Systems

    Urban infrastructure still depends on outdated OT systems that can't be easily patched or integrated with modern cybersecurity. These legacy systems create critical vulnerabilities that attackers can exploit—causing service disruptions and safety risks.

  • IT & OT Convergence

    Merging IT and OT boosts operational efficiency but broadens the attack surface. A breach in IT can enable attackers to move laterally into OT systems—leading to control over physical infrastructure and endangering lives.

  • Complex Interconnectivity

    City systems like energy, water, and transport are interconnected. A cyberattack on one can trigger cascading failures across others. This high interdependence demands unified, cross-domain cybersecurity strategies.

  • Human Risk Factors

    Insiders—employees or contractors—can unintentionally introduce threats via phishing, poor password hygiene, or misconfigured systems. Human error remains one of the leading causes of cyber incidents.

  • Regulatory Compliance

    Urban infrastructure sectors face a maze of regional and sector-specific regulations. Staying compliant is costly and complex, but non-compliance can lead to heavy penalties and greater cyber exposure.
